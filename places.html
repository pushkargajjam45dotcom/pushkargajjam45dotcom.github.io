Siddheshwar Temple

Siddheshwar Temple is one of the most important spiritual landmarks in Solapur[cite: 15]. Dedicated to Lord Siddheshwar, a form of Lord Shiva, it is located near the peaceful Siddheshwar Lake in the center of the city[cite: 16]. It becomes especially crowded during the Siddheshwar Yatra festival every January[cite: 17].

Pandharpur

Located about 70 km from Solapur, Pandharpur is one of the most sacred pilgrimage towns in Maharashtra[cite: 19]. It is famous for the Vitthal Rukmini Temple dedicated to Lord Vitthal[cite: 20]. Millions of devotees participate in the Wari pilgrimage during the Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi festivals[cite: 21].

Tuljapur Pilgrimage

Solapur serves as a gateway to several nearby pilgrimage destinations that attract millions of devotees every year[cite: 7, 11]. These destinations are easily accessible by road from the city[cite: 72].

Akkalkot

Located about 40 km from Solapur, Akkalkot is famous for the temple of Shri Swami Samarth Maharaj[cite: 23, 24]. The temple atmosphere is calm and peaceful, attracting visitors from all over the country[cite: 26].

Solapur Fort

Also called Bhuikot Fort, this historic monument was built during the Bahmani Sultanate period[cite: 28, 29]. The structure reflects the military architecture of medieval India and represents the rich historical heritage of Solapur[cite: 30].
